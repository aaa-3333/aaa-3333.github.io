British Book Awards

  • Children's Book of the Year: Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone won this award in 1997.
  • Book of the Year: Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets won this award in 1998.

Hugo Award

Best Novel Nomination: Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire was nominated for the Hugo Award in 2001.

Nestle Smarties Book Prize

Gold Medal (9-11 Age Category): Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone won this award in 1997.

American Library Association Awards

Best Book for Young Adults: Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban won this award in 1999.

National Book Awards (UK)

Children's Book of the Year: Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire won this award in 2000.

Hans Christian Andersen Award

Special Recognition: J.K. Rowling received this honor in 2010 for her contribution to children's literature through the Harry Potter series.

Other Honors

  • Order of the British Empire (OBE): J.K. Rowling was awarded the OBE in 2000 for her services to literature.
  • Edinburgh Award: J.K. Rowling received this award in 2008 in recognition of her contributions to the city of Edinburgh.

Film Awards

  • Academy Awards (Oscars): The film series received multiple nominations, with Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows - Part 2 earning nominations for Best Art Direction, Best Visual Effects, and Best Makeup.
  • BAFTA Awards: The film series received numerous nominations and awards.

Sales Records

Global Sales: As of 2021, the Harry Potter series has sold over 500 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling book series in history.
